What we have noticed with the data? Based on our data gathering and research.

  • Making more roads doesn't really solve the problem.
  • Reducing the number of people commuting to work daily does not change anything.
  • Better results are obtained when the amount cars used to commute daily are controlled.
  • Doing nothing will only result in taking drastic actions in future.
  • Building more transport infrastructure to fix road congestion without managing demand is financially and environmentally unsustainable.
  • Pricing can spread demand, improve the efficiency and reliability of travel, reduce the costs of goods and services, reduce emissions and improve amenity.

How we can reduce the traffic problem? See for yourself.

Our Goal: Reduce the number of cars on the road.

People dont want to give up using there cars, so how do we convince pople to use other modes of transport?

simple.. a pay as you use model that incurrages people to take passengers or seek alternative transportation to avoid extra costs.

Step 1: Change the outer roads around the cbd into a massive 6 lane roundabout.
Step 2: Install cameras at the entry points to the cbd and implement a toll system where you get charged a entry fee unless you are comuting with a passenger.
Step 3: Break melbourne up into zones and install cameras that charge people when they drive across zones without a passenger.
Step 4: Setup a priceing structure that changes depending on traffic conditions Higher during peek hour for example
